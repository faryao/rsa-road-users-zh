# Wheelchair accessible vehicles

### A Wheelchair Accessible Vehicle (WAV) is a passenger vehicle converted to accommodate persons seated in their wheelchairs.

This content is for general information only. It does not, and is not intended to, provide legal or technical advice or to represent a legal interpretation of the matters it addresses.

* * *

## Frequently asked questions

[What is the definition of a wheelchair accessible vehicle?](http://www.rsa.ie/road-safety/road-users/special-purpose-vehicles/wheelchair-accessible-vehicles#)

It is defined in[Regulation (EU) No.2018/858](https://eur-lex.europa.eu/legal-content/EN/TXT/PDF/?uri=CELEX:32018R0858) as: ‘a vehicle of category M1 constructed or converted specifically so that they accommodate one or more persons seated in their wheelchairs when travelling on the road’.

[What is the definition of a disabled person’s vehicle?](http://www.rsa.ie/road-safety/road-users/special-purpose-vehicles/wheelchair-accessible-vehicles#)

It is defined under [S.I. No. 556 of 2020](http://www.irishstatutebook.ie/eli/2020/si/556/made/en/print?q=556&years=2020) as ‘a vehicle that is adapted or specially constructed so as to enable a person who has a disability to travel in the vehicle, whether as the driver or a passenger in safety and reasonable comfort’.

[Do wheelchair accessible vehicles require type-approval?](http://www.rsa.ie/road-safety/road-users/special-purpose-vehicles/wheelchair-accessible-vehicles#)

Since 29 April 2012, all new special purpose vehicles of category M1 will require type-approval in order to be registered in Ireland. Vehicles that are categorised as SPVs include wheelchair accessible passenger cars and disabled persons vehicles. For further information, visit [Revenue](https://www.revenue.ie/en/search.aspx?q=special%20purpose%20vehicles). You may also refer to [type-approval](http://www.rsa.ie/road-safety/road-users/legislation) for special purpose vehicles.

[Who do I contact if I have adapted my vehicle and need to get tax relief and vehicle registration tax (VRT) rate changed?](http://www.rsa.ie/road-safety/road-users/special-purpose-vehicles/wheelchair-accessible-vehicles#)

Any queries regarding the payment or refund of VRT should be referred to [Revenue](https://www.revenue.ie/en/search.aspx?q=vehicle%20registration%20tax).

[Do wheelchair accessible vehicles require registration, tax, testing and insurance?](http://www.rsa.ie/road-safety/road-users/special-purpose-vehicles/wheelchair-accessible-vehicles#)

Yes. All mechanically propelled vehicles must be registered, taxed, tested and insured in order to be used on a public road.

[Who do I contact as a wheelchair user to adapt a vehicle so that I can drive it?](http://www.rsa.ie/road-safety/road-users/special-purpose-vehicles/wheelchair-accessible-vehicles#)

Modifications must be carried out by a suitably qualified individual at an approved test centre to an appropriate and safe standard. See our FAQ on [Vehicle Modifications](http://www.rsa.ie/road-safety/road-users/car-drivers/vehicle-modifications).

Contact Irish Wheelchair Association ( [IWA](http://www.iwa.ie/services/motoring)) and [Revenue](http://www.revenue.ie/en/importing-vehicles-duty-free-allowances/guide-to-vrt/reliefs-and-exemptions/scheme-for-persons-with-disabilities.aspx) regarding adaptations.

You may also wish to contact [NSAI](https://www.nsai.ie/).

[I purchased a van and converted it to a wheelchair accessible passenger vehicle. Where do I test it?](http://www.rsa.ie/road-safety/road-users/special-purpose-vehicles/wheelchair-accessible-vehicles#)

The roadworthiness test is based on the vehicle category not on the use of the vehicle or whether it is registered as private or commercial.

A vehicle category is assigned depending on the number of seats in a vehicle, it's goods carrying capacity and its weight.
