# Special purpose vehicles

### Vehicle registration, type-approval, roadworthiness testing.

This content is for general information only. It does not, and is not intended to, provide legal or technical advice or to represent a legal interpretation of the matters it addresses.

* * *

## Frequently asked questions

[What are special purpose vehicles (SPVs)?](http://www.rsa.ie/road-safety/road-users/special-purpose-vehicles/special-purpose-vehicles#)

These vehicles belong either to M (passenger vehicles) , N (goods vehicles) or O (trailers) category vehicles having specific technical features in order to perform a function which require special arrangements and or equipment.

[Do SPVs require type approval?](http://www.rsa.ie/road-safety/road-users/special-purpose-vehicles/special-purpose-vehicles#)

Since 29 April 2012, all NEW special purpose M1 vehicles must have proof of type-approval before Revenue will register them for use on Irish roads. The manufacturer may apply for [type-approval](http://www.rsa.ie/road-safety/road-users/legislation)or individual vehicle approval for vehicles that are designed and constructed for use by the Defence Forces, Civil Defence, Fire Services or the Gardai.

[Do SPVs require roadworthiness testing?](http://www.rsa.ie/road-safety/road-users/special-purpose-vehicles/special-purpose-vehicles#)

Yes. The only SPVs exempt from roadworthiness testing are those used by An Garda Síochána and the Defence Forces.

[What is the registration procedure for SPVs?](http://www.rsa.ie/road-safety/road-users/special-purpose-vehicles/special-purpose-vehicles#)

You will need to contact [Revenue Open in new browser tab Open in new browser tab](https://www.revenue.ie/en/importing-vehicles-duty-free-allowances/guide-to-vrt/vehicle-registration-tax/index.aspx) who is responsible for vehicle registration in Ireland.

[Is a first aid kit a legal requirement for minibuses when transporting patients to and from home?](http://www.rsa.ie/road-safety/road-users/special-purpose-vehicles/special-purpose-vehicles#)

A vehicle with more than 8 passengers is required to have a first aid kit.

[What is the definition of an ambulance?](http://www.rsa.ie/road-safety/road-users/special-purpose-vehicles/special-purpose-vehicles#)

An ambulance has EU body type code SC and is defined as ‘a vehicle of category M intended for the transport of sick or injured persons and having special equipment for such purpose’. The patient compartment must comply with the technical requirements of [Standard I.S. EN 1789:2020 + A1:2023 Open in new browser tab Open in new browser tab](https://shop.standards.ie/en-ie/standards/i-s-en-1789-2020-a1-2023-860875_saig_nsai_nsai_3364930/). which specifies the requirements for design, testing, performance, and equipping of road ambulances capable of transporting at least one person on a stretcher.

[Do ambulances require type approval?](http://www.rsa.ie/road-safety/road-users/special-purpose-vehicles/special-purpose-vehicles#)

Yes. Since 29 April 2012, all NEW special purpose M1 vehicles must have proof of type-approval.

[Do ambulances require roadworthiness testing?](http://www.rsa.ie/road-safety/road-users/special-purpose-vehicles/special-purpose-vehicles#)

Yes. They are required to be tested at [CVRT](http://www.rsa.ie/services/vehicle-owners/commercial-vehicle-roadworthiness-test-(cvrt)) Centres.
