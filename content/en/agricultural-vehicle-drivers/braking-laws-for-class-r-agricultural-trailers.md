# Trailer braking laws

### Maximum design speeds, braking efficiency checks and manual slack adjusters.

* * *

The braking requirements for class R agricultural trailers or towed equipment manufactured **on or after** 1 January 2016 are outlined in the table below.

| Maximum design speed of trailer is 40 km/h and DGVW* is less than 5 tonnes | Design speed of trailer is over 40 km/h & DGVW* is 5 tonnes or more |
| --- | --- |
| Service Brake - minimum brake efficiency of 25% | Service Brake - minimum brake efficiency of 45% |
| Parking Brake - minimum brake efficiency of 16% | Parking Brake - minimum brake efficiency of 16% |
| Breakaway brake - minimum brake efficiency of 13.5% | Breakaway brake - minimum brake efficiency of 13.5% |

*DGVW is the design gross vehicle weight, also referred to MAM (maximum authorised mass) on the trailer's plate is the weight of a vehicle or trailer including the maximum load it can carry as per the manufacturer's design specifications.

*   Trailers with a design speed of over 40 km/h that have been manufactured on or after 01 January 2016 must also be equipped with pneumatic braking systems including load-sensing capability.
*   Trailers with a design speed of over 60 km/h that have been manufactured on or after 01 January 2016 must be equipped with anti-lock braking systems (ABS).
*   Older trailers, provided they are designed to be drawn at speeds of 40 km/h or less can be fitted with a secondary coupling e.g., a chain or wire rope instead of a breakaway brake.

The braking requirements for class R agricultural trailers manufactured **before** 1 January 2016 are outlined in the table below.

| Maximum design speed is 40 km/h | Design speed of trailer is over 40 km/h |
| --- | --- |
| Must be fitted with a service brake, a breakaway brake or fitted with a secondary coupling (consisting of a chain or wire rope) | Service Brake – 45% performance Breakaway Brake – 13.5% performance Parking – 16% performance |

## Frequently asked questions

[What if my trailer or piece of interchangeable towed equipment cannot be fitted with an anti-lock braking system (ABS)?](http://www.rsa.ie/road-safety/road-users/agricultural/braking-laws-for-class-r-agricultural-trailers-and-towed-equipment#)

If your trailer or interchangeable towed equipment was manufactured before 01 January 2016, there is no requirement to retrofit ABS.

[What are ABS requirements for agricultural trailers designed to be drawn at a speed exceeding 60 km/h?](http://www.rsa.ie/road-safety/road-users/agricultural/braking-laws-for-class-r-agricultural-trailers-and-towed-equipment#)

Every new agricultural trailer manufactured on or after 01 January 2016 which is designed to be drawn at a speed over 60 km/h must be fitted with ABS complying with the construction and performance requirements of the General Safety Regulation[2019/2144 Open in new browser tab Open in new browser tab](https://eur-lex.europa.eu/legal-content/EN/TXT/PDF/?uri=CELEX:32019R2144&from=EN) as amended or of UN/ECE regulation 13.09. It is important to note that where the DGVW of the agricultural trailer is more than 10,000 kg a category A anti-lock braking system must be fitted. This is an ABS that controls the braking of the wheels independently on each side of the trailer.

[I want to upgrade the brakes on my trailer so that I can operate at speeds over 40 km/h. What should I do?](http://www.rsa.ie/road-safety/road-users/agricultural/braking-laws-for-class-r-agricultural-trailers-and-towed-equipment#)

You should contact the original trailer manufacturer or authorised Irish distributor. If the original manufacturer is no longer in business, you could contact another manufacturer of similar equipment who may be able to examine the vehicle to see what work if any needs to be done to achieve compliance for operation at speeds over 40 km/h.

[Where can I get the braking efficiency checked on the agricultural trailers I manufacture?](http://www.rsa.ie/road-safety/road-users/agricultural/braking-laws-for-class-r-agricultural-trailers-and-towed-equipment#)

You can take the vehicle to your local commercial vehicle roadworthiness test centre ([CVRT Open in new browser tab Open in new browser tab](https://www.cvrt.ie/en-US/Find-a-test-centre/))and get a brake test done. There will likely be a fee for this and is a private arrangement between you and the test centre.

[Is it ok for trailers or towed equipment with load-sensing devices to display settings on the plate for just two loading conditions i.e., unladen and laden?](http://www.rsa.ie/road-safety/road-users/agricultural/braking-laws-for-class-r-agricultural-trailers-and-towed-equipment#)

Yes, once it is set automatically.

[Can trailers or pieces of interchangeable towed equipment be fitted with manual slack adjusters?](http://www.rsa.ie/road-safety/road-users/agricultural/braking-laws-for-class-r-agricultural-trailers-and-towed-equipment#)

Yes, but only for trailers or towed equipment with design speeds of 40 km/h or less or manufactured after 01 January 2016. Those with design speeds over 40 km/h and with a DGVW of more than 3.5 tonnes must be fitted with a pneumatic braking system and automatic slack adjusters whether or not a hydraulic braking system is also fitted. This is in compliance with the construction and performance requirements of the General Safety Regulation [2019/2144 Open in new browser tab Open in new browser tab](https://eur-lex.europa.eu/legal-content/EN/TXT/PDF/?uri=CELEX:32019R2144&from=EN) as amended.

[My trailer was never equipped with brakes and even if it had my tractor has no way of powering them. What do I need to do?](http://www.rsa.ie/road-safety/road-users/agricultural/braking-laws-for-class-r-agricultural-trailers-and-towed-equipment#)

If your tractor is designed to travel at speeds of 40km/h or less, you can draw a trailer with a laden weight of 5 tonnes or less without brakes. This is only the case if when laden the trailer does not exceed three times the tractor’s unladen weight.

Note: If your tractor is designed to travel at speeds over 40 km/h you can only draw a trailer with a laden weight of 3.5 tonnes without brakes
