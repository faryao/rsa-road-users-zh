# Wheelchair restraints

### The use of wheelchair restraints in vehicles.

This content is for general information only. It does not, and is not intended to, provide legal or technical advice or to represent a legal interpretation of the matters it addresses.

* * *

## Frequently asked questions

[What is the definition of person with a disabilities’ seat belt?](http://www.rsa.ie/road-safety/road-users/vehicle-components/vehicle-components/seatbelts-and-child-restraints/wheelchair-restraints#)

It is defined as ‘a safety belt which has been specifically designed or adapted for use by a person with a physical handicap or disability and which is intended for use solely by such a person.’

[What is the law in relation to wheelchairs in vehicles?](http://www.rsa.ie/road-safety/road-users/vehicle-components/vehicle-components/seatbelts-and-child-restraints/wheelchair-restraints#)

Wheelchairs must be securely attached to the vehicle using proper tie-down designed to keep the wheelchair restrained within the wheelchair space and occupant restraint systems so that no danger is likely to be caused. The same applies to securing disability aids e.g., walking sticks.

[Do wheelchair passengers have to wear seat belts?](http://www.rsa.ie/road-safety/road-users/vehicle-components/vehicle-components/seatbelts-and-child-restraints/wheelchair-restraints#)

No. A person who is wearing a belt as part of a wheelchair restraint system and or holds a medical cert from a registered medical practitioner does not have to wear a regular seat belt.

[Who can advise on the proper installation of wheelchair restraints?](http://www.rsa.ie/road-safety/road-users/vehicle-components/vehicle-components/seatbelts-and-child-restraints/wheelchair-restraints#)

The National Standards Authority of Ireland ([NSAI](https://www.nsai.ie/)) can provide more specific guidance on recommended or approved standards for the installation of wheelchair restraints in brand new unregistered vehicles. Only type-approved wheelchair restraints can be installed and this can only be done by a restraints manufacturer.

[Is training available for handling or operation of wheelchair restraints?](http://www.rsa.ie/road-safety/road-users/vehicle-components/vehicle-components/seatbelts-and-child-restraints/wheelchair-restraints#)

Yes. You can contact The Irish Wheelchair Association ([IWA](http://www.iwa.ie/)) at 090 647 1118 or email [athlone@iwa.ie.](mailto:athlone@iwa.ie)

[How do I get certification for wheelchair restraints or wheelchair lifts?](http://www.rsa.ie/road-safety/road-users/vehicle-components/vehicle-components/seatbelts-and-child-restraints/wheelchair-restraints#)

The ([NSAI](https://www.nsai.ie/)) can provide more specific guidance on recommended or approval standards for the installation of wheelchair restraints in brand new or unregistered vehicles. Only type-approved wheelchair restraints can be installed and this approval can only be obtained by restraints manufacturer.

[How can I remove seats from a new unregistered vehicle to accommodate wheelchair or stretcher passengers?](http://www.rsa.ie/road-safety/road-users/vehicle-components/vehicle-components/seatbelts-and-child-restraints/wheelchair-restraints#)

Step 1. Contact the original manufacturer or authorised distributor to find out if it is possible to carry out these modifications or changes and whether there are any safety implications.

Step 2.Contact[](https://www.revenue.ie/en/vrt/guide-to-vrt/conversions/index.aspx)**[Revenue](https://www.revenue.ie/en/Home.aspx)**to see if there are any VRT implications. If there are no VRT implications, you will need to contact your local[](https://www.motortax.ie/OMT/welcome.do)**[motor tax office](https://www.motortax.ie/OMT/welcome.do)**.

[Are wheelchair restraints checked at roadworthiness test?](http://www.rsa.ie/road-safety/road-users/vehicle-components/vehicle-components/seatbelts-and-child-restraints/wheelchair-restraints#)

Yes.

*   If your vehicle is a bus, you will need seat belt certification.
*   If your vehicle has been physically modified since its last roadworthiness test then you will also need to present a modifications report that can be downloaded from [NCT Testers' Manual](http://www.rsa.ie/docs/default-source/road-safety/nct/national-car-test-(nct)-manual-2023.pdf?sfvrsn=b59cc4cf_3) for passenger vehicles or [CVRT Testers' Manuals](https://www.cvrt.ie/en-US/About-CVRT/New-CVR-Testing-Manuals/) for commercial vehicles.
*   A modifications report has to be certified by an approved test centre or suitably qualified individual e.g. mechanical engineer on [Engineers Ireland](https://www.engineersireland.ie/Professionals) or [SIMI](https://www.simi.ie/en/contact-simi).

[Do I have to fit head restraints for wheelchairs on buses?](http://www.rsa.ie/road-safety/road-users/vehicle-components/vehicle-components/seatbelts-and-child-restraints/wheelchair-restraints#)

No. There is currently no legal requirement for this. However, we recommend that wheelchair tie- down and occupant-restraint systems meet with the requirements of ISO 10542; and that there is wheelchair back support solutions incorporating a headrest available to fit to vehicles equipped with appropriate floor tracking systems. For more information, refer to [UN-ECE-R107](https://op.europa.eu/en/publication-detail/-/publication/d8730fbc-157e-11e5-8817-01aa75ed71a1).

[Should I install child locks on a bus for people with learning disabilities?](http://www.rsa.ie/road-safety/road-users/vehicle-components/vehicle-components/seatbelts-and-child-restraints/wheelchair-restraints#)

This is not a legal requirement for transporting persons with a disability. If in the event of a collision the driver became incapacitated and the occupants would encounter difficulties exiting the vehicle, we would recommend installing buzzers which would alert the driver should one of the rear doors open either accidently or otherwise.

[I modified my vehicle for wheelchair access. Where do I apply for a VRT rebate or discount?](http://www.rsa.ie/road-safety/road-users/vehicle-components/vehicle-components/seatbelts-and-child-restraints/wheelchair-restraints#)

You will need to contact [Revenue](http://www.revenue.ie/en/importing-vehicles-duty-free-allowances/guide-to-vrt/conversions/conversion.aspx) to enquire whether you need to declare the conversion and whether changes to your vehicle registration tax (VRT) may apply.

[Is it a requirement for bus drivers to have specialised training for wheelchair clamping on buses?](http://www.rsa.ie/road-safety/road-users/vehicle-components/vehicle-components/seatbelts-and-child-restraints/wheelchair-restraints#)

This will depend on who is tasked with clamping down the wheelchairs e.g., driver or assistant. From the point of view of training for work, you should contact the Health and Safety Authority ([HSA](https://www.hsa.ie/eng/)) for further advice.
