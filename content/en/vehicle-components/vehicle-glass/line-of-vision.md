# Line of vision

### Vehicle glass, glazing and line of vision.

This content is for general information only. It does not, and is not intended to, provide legal or technical advice or to represent a legal interpretation of the matters it addresses.

* * *

## Frequently asked questions

[What are the legal levels of transparency for windscreens and front side windows?](http://www.rsa.ie/road-safety/road-users/vehicle-components/vehicle-glass/line-of-vision#)

*   Under EU type-approval regulations, all newly manufactured vehicles must have a minimum light transparency of 70% on the windscreen and front side windows.
*   Under roadworthiness testing regulations, 
*   Glass in windscreen, internal panes/partitioning and front side windows located in a position requisite for driving has a light transmission level of less than 65%.

Darkening a vehicle’s windscreen more than this or increasing the size of a vehicle’s sunblock would be illegal. For further information, see [NCT Testers' Manual](http://www.rsa.ie/services/vehicle-owners/national-car-test-(nct)/about-the-national-car-test-(nct)).

[Is there a law against having a cracked windscreen?](http://www.rsa.ie/road-safety/road-users/vehicle-components/vehicle-glass/line-of-vision#)

 If your vehicle’s windscreen is likely to produce fragments capable of causing severe cuts if fractured, then it is illegal under road traffic regulations and should be replaced. 

[What are the laws on using stickers or advertising on a vehicle’s windows?](http://www.rsa.ie/road-safety/road-users/vehicle-components/vehicle-glass/line-of-vision#)

 Under road traffic law, if the driver’s view of the road and other traffic – either to the front or side of the vehicle - is limited or distorted by an object e.g., sticker, signage etc. that prevents them from driving safely, it is illegal and should be removed. 

[What can I do if I had a new windscreen fitted and it is causing the sun to reflect items onto it limiting my vision while driving?](http://www.rsa.ie/road-safety/road-users/vehicle-components/vehicle-glass/line-of-vision#)

 You should contact the company who fitted the windscreen. If they will not replace it, you can contact the competition and consumer protection commission ([CCPC Open in new browser tab Open in new browser tab](https://www.ccpc.ie/)) for advice on your rights as a consumer. 

[What standard of glass is required to pass roadworthiness test?](http://www.rsa.ie/road-safety/road-users/vehicle-components/vehicle-glass/line-of-vision#)

For information on Methods of Testing and Reasons for Failure, see the following:

[NCT Testers' Manual](http://www.rsa.ie/services/vehicle-owners/national-car-test-(nct)/about-the-national-car-test-(nct)) – Passenger Cars.

[CVRT Testers' Manual Open in new browser tab Open in new browser tab](https://www.cvrt.ie/en-US/About-CVRT/New-CVR-Testing-Manuals/) – Heavy and Light Commercial Vehicles.

[Will my vehicle fail the test if e-mark is not visible?](http://www.rsa.ie/road-safety/road-users/vehicle-components/vehicle-glass/line-of-vision#)

 Yes. If windscreens, side, or rear windows are fitted with glazing material other than safety glass, the vehicle owner must provide certification from the manufacturer or installer stating that the glazing material is not likely if fractured to produce fragments capable of causing severe cuts. See [LCV/HCV Testers' Manual Open in new browser tab Open in new browser tab](https://www.cvrt.ie/en-US/About-CVRT/New-CVR-Testing-Manuals/) or [NCT Testers' Manual](http://www.rsa.ie/services/vehicle-owners/national-car-test-(nct)/about-the-national-car-test-(nct))or if you are still unsure, contact your local test centre who may be able to provide testing advice. 

[If the glazing material is not safety glass will it fail the test?](http://www.rsa.ie/road-safety/road-users/vehicle-components/vehicle-glass/line-of-vision#)

 Yes. Unless it can be verified from the manufacturer or glass installer stating that the glazing material is not likely if fractured to produce fragments capable of causing severe cuts. 

[Is it illegal to fit a sun strip on the windscreen of my car as the sun visor is too high to have any effect?](http://www.rsa.ie/road-safety/road-users/vehicle-components/vehicle-glass/line-of-vision#)

Yes.

*   Under Roadworthiness Testing laws [S.I. No 415 of 2017 Open in new browser tab Open in new browser tab](http://www.irishstatutebook.ie/eli/2017/si/415/made/en/pdf), a vehicle’s windscreen and front side windows must have a light transmission transparency level of at least 65% to pass the test. If a tinted sun strip comes down below the tip of the wipers, it can impair the driver’s vision and prevent them from having a clear view of the road. It also constitutes a failure item at the vehicle’s test in [NCT Testers' Manual](http://www.rsa.ie/services/vehicle-owners/national-car-test-(nct)/about-the-national-car-test-(nct)).
*   Under Irish Road Traffic regulations, the driver must always have a view to the front and side of the vehicle necessary to enable them to drive safely. Heavily tinted windows present a significant safety hazard for vehicle drivers and their occupants. Visibility is greatly reduced, particularly at night-time or times of low light, and may prevent drivers from seeing other road users or pedestrians.

[How can a solid sun visor covering most of a windscreen be acceptable at NCT and a transparent visor sun strip across the top of the windscreen unacceptable?](http://www.rsa.ie/road-safety/road-users/vehicle-components/vehicle-glass/line-of-vision#)

*   A sun visor is a temporary measure to prevent strong sunlight blinding the driver. It can be folded away when not required and therefore does not restrict a driver’s view of the road during times of low light.
*   A tinted sun strip remains permanently on the windscreen and if it is wide enough to come down below the top of the wipers it is considered to be in the driver’s line of the vision and therefore can restrict their view of the road especially during times of poor light. For further information, on 'Reasons for Failure' refer to [NCT Testers' Manual](http://www.rsa.ie/services/vehicle-owners/national-car-test-(nct)/about-the-national-car-test-(nct)).
