# Car maintenance

### Advice and information about maintaining your car in a safe and roadworthy condition.

You must maintain your car regularly and ensure it’s always in a proper working condition. This is [the law Open in new browser tab](http://www.irishstatutebook.ie/eli/1961/act/24/enacted/en/html) and there are penalties for anyone who drives an unroadworthy vehicle in a public place. What’s more, you shouldn’t let the condition of your car put your safety, and the safety of your passengers or other road users at risk.

## National Car Test (NCT)

The [National Car Test (NCT)Open in new browser tab](https://www.ncts.ie/) is a preventative road safety measure that ensures cars using Irish roads are subject to a basic safety check at regular intervals.

![Image 31](http://www.rsa.ie/images/default-source/in-page-full-width-(868-x-487)/vehicle-inspection.jpg?Status=Master&sfvrsn=6f3f49f5_3)

The NCT confirms that a vehicle satisfies basic safety standards on the day the test is carried out. It tests only what is accessible and visible. Therefore, it is not a warranty as to the condition of the vehicle.

The NCT is not meant to replace the regular maintenance that a mechanic needs to carry out on a vehicle.

[About the NCT Information about the National Car Test (NCT) certificate. Replacing a NCT certificate. Certificates when importing a car from an EU state. Odometer readings. Learn more](http://www.rsa.ie/services/vehicle-owners/national-car-test-(nct)/about-the-national-car-test-(nct))
