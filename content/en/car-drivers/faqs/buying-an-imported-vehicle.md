# Buying an imported vehicle

### Things to be aware of when buying an imported vehicle.

This content is for general information only. It does not, and is not intended to, provide legal or technical advice or to represent a legal interpretation of the matters it addresses.

* * *

## Frequently asked questions

[What are the regulations on vehicle importation, VRT payment and registration?](http://www.rsa.ie/road-safety/road-users/car-drivers/resources/buying-an-imported-vehicle#)

When importing a vehicle an appointment should be made with Revenue to have the vehicle inspected within seven days of it entering the state in order to register it and pay VRT. The registration process must be completed within 30 days of entry into the state. For further information on importation, VRT and registration contact [Revenue Open in new browser tab Open in new browser tab](https://www.revenue.ie/en/importing-vehicles-duty-free-allowances/guide-to-vrt/vehicle-registration-tax/vrt-and-registration.aspx).

[How do I check the validity of a roadworthiness cert on a UK imported vehicle?](http://www.rsa.ie/road-safety/road-users/car-drivers/resources/buying-an-imported-vehicle#)

Input the vehicle make and registration into the [MOT History Check Open in new browser tab Open in new browser tab](https://www.gov.uk/check-mot-history)

[Can I import a vehicle without a valid roadworthiness cert?](http://www.rsa.ie/road-safety/road-users/car-drivers/resources/buying-an-imported-vehicle#)

Yes. However, once the vehicle is registered a valid roadworthiness test must be carried out.

[Can I transfer insurance onto a vehicle without a valid roadworthiness cert?](http://www.rsa.ie/road-safety/road-users/car-drivers/resources/buying-an-imported-vehicle#)

Contact your insurance provider or [Insurance Ireland Open in new browser tab Open in new browser tab](https://www.insuranceireland.eu/consumer-information).

[If I import a vehicle from an EU member state with a valid roadworthiness cert can I use it on Irish roads?](http://www.rsa.ie/road-safety/road-users/car-drivers/resources/buying-an-imported-vehicle#)

Yes. Since 20 May 2018 you can now exchange a valid roadworthiness certificate from any EU member state. However, please note, as a result of Brexit, the mutual recognition of UK-issued certificates of roadworthiness is no longer accepted.

For further information on mutual recognition of roadworthiness certs for cars visit [NCT Open in new browser tab Open in new browser tab](https://www.ncts.ie/) and for commercial vehicles visit [CVRT Open in new browser tab Open in new browser tab](https://www.cvrt.ie/en-US/Certificate-of-Roadworthiness/Recognition-of-EU-Certificates/).

[If I purchase a car in Northern Ireland or UK and transfer my insurance, am I insured to drive it here?](http://www.rsa.ie/road-safety/road-users/car-drivers/resources/buying-an-imported-vehicle#)

Contact your insurance provider for details relating to your insurance policy.

[I am importing a car from Japan which will be subject to new NOx tax introduced on 01 January 2020. Do RSA have NOx ratings?](http://www.rsa.ie/road-safety/road-users/car-drivers/resources/buying-an-imported-vehicle#)

Nitrogen Oxide (NOx) tax falls under the remit of Revenue and was added to the VRT calculation on 1 January 2020. The calculation of the NOx charge is outlined on their [website Open in new browser tab Open in new browser tab](https://www.revenue.ie/en/vrt/guide-to-vrt/calculating-vrt/calculating-nox-charge.aspx).
