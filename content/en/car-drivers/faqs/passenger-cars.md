# Passenger cars

### Information and frequently asked questions on passenger cars.

This content is for general information only. It does not, and is not intended to, provide legal or technical advice or to represent a legal interpretation of the matters it addresses.

* * *

## Frequently asked questions

[What is the maximum number of passenger seats a passenger car or people carrier (M1) can have?](http://www.rsa.ie/road-safety/road-users/car-drivers/resources/passenger-cars#)

These vehicles are designed and constructed for the carriage of passengers, comprising of no more than eight seats in addition to the driver.

[Are there regulations for carrying a bike rack on the back of a car?](http://www.rsa.ie/road-safety/road-users/car-drivers/resources/passenger-cars#)

If a bicycle carrier or other similar attachment were to obscure the lights and reflectors required to be fitted to the rear of a vehicle, then a light board or lamps should be fitted to the attachment itself.

[Where can I obtain garage trade plates?](http://www.rsa.ie/road-safety/road-users/car-drivers/resources/passenger-cars#)

You can email [motortax@transport.gov.ie.](mailto:motortax@transport.gov.ie)

[Can I fit a tow bar on a car?](http://www.rsa.ie/road-safety/road-users/car-drivers/resources/passenger-cars#)

You will need to contact the manufacturer or authorised distributor to see if your vehicle is designed to do so and to ensure that it is fitted correctly.

[If a car is off the road and not taxed, is motor tax payable from date of purchase and getting the logbook?](http://www.rsa.ie/road-safety/road-users/car-drivers/resources/passenger-cars#)

You will need to contact your local [motor tax office Open in new browser tab Open in new browser tab](https://www.gov.ie/en/service/1fc151-find-your-local-motor-tax-office/).

[Is it a requirement to carry a first aid kit in a car?](http://www.rsa.ie/road-safety/road-users/car-drivers/resources/passenger-cars#)

No. However we recommend carrying a breakdown warning triangle, first aid kit, fire extinguisher and spare bulb kit in your vehicle.

[What is the speed limit for vehicles on Irish roads?](http://www.rsa.ie/road-safety/road-users/car-drivers/resources/passenger-cars#)

You will need to refer to Speed Limits section of[Rules of the Road (English)](http://www.rsa.ie/docs/default-source/services/s1.8-learner-driver-resources/rules_of_the_road.pdf?Status=Master&sfvrsn=723cf1ac_39 "Rules of the Road (English)")

[Is it possible to register a new vehicle in Ireland when speedometer is in miles?](http://www.rsa.ie/road-safety/road-users/car-drivers/resources/passenger-cars#)

The law requires that new vehicles must use metric units i.e., km on the speedometer.

[Are speed limiters required in new cars?](http://www.rsa.ie/road-safety/road-users/car-drivers/resources/passenger-cars#)

Passenger cars are not currently within this scope. Intelligent speed assistance (ISA) technology has been mandated in some new vehicle models since July 2022.

[Is there an approved car rental company I should use when renting a car in Ireland?](http://www.rsa.ie/road-safety/road-users/car-drivers/resources/passenger-cars#)

It is advisable to make your reservation directly with a car rental company approved by the [Car Rental Council of Ireland.Open in new browser tab Open in new browser tab](http://www.carrentalcouncil.ie/) Any complaint with respect to your car rental service should be lodged with this council.

[Who do I contact if I have a complaint regarding a car rental company in Europe?](http://www.rsa.ie/road-safety/road-users/car-drivers/resources/passenger-cars#)

You can contact European Consumer Centre Ireland ( [ECCI Open in new browser tab Open in new browser tab](https://www.eccireland.ie/faqs/car-rental/)).

[Is there a specific standard of safety that rental cars should meet?](http://www.rsa.ie/road-safety/road-users/car-drivers/resources/passenger-cars#)

Yes. All vehicles in use in a public place must conform to [Construction, Equipment and Use Regulations Open in new browser tab Open in new browser tab](http://www.irishstatutebook.ie/eli/1963/si/190/made/en/print). In summary the vehicle shall be maintained in a roadworthy condition at all times and must not present a danger to any road user.

[Do I need to check the car before I hire it?](http://www.rsa.ie/road-safety/road-users/car-drivers/resources/passenger-cars#)

Yes. We would always advise that you carry out a [walk around check](http://www.rsa.ie/docs/default-source/road-safety/r1.4-car-drivers/rsa-12-car-safety-checks.pdf?sfvrsn=83c94cea_3) before driving any vehicle. Always check that there is valid insurance and tax and if the vehicle is over four years that there is a valid NCT disc displayed on the windscreen.

[Is it legal to carry luggage on your vehicle’s roof rack?](http://www.rsa.ie/road-safety/road-users/car-drivers/resources/passenger-cars#)

Yes, provided the roof rack or box is compatible with the vehicle and any load fitted is securely attached with no possibility of it falling off. You should also ensure that you do not exceed the design capacity of the roof rack or the design gross weight of the vehicle as set out by the manufacturer.

[Is it permissible to carry pets in cars?](http://www.rsa.ie/road-safety/road-users/car-drivers/resources/passenger-cars#)

Yes, however under the Road Traffic Construction, Equipment and Use of Vehicles regulations animals are classed as a ‘load’ in a vehicle and therefore must be properly secured and not present a danger to any person. In addition, the ‘load’ must not impede the driver’s control over the vehicle or their view of the road. For more detailed information on the securement of loads refer to [S.I. No. 190 of 1963 Open in new browser tab Open in new browser tab](http://www.irishstatutebook.ie/eli/1963/si/190/made/en/print) under Section 92 ‘General duties of drivers and others and Article 96 General provisions.

We recommend that you secure your pet safely on any journey so that it cannot distract or hinder the driver in any way. To ensure passenger and animal safety, pets should be contained within a travelling crate or container. It is also advisable to use a safety grid or mesh.
