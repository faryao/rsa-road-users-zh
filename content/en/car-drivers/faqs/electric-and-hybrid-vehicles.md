# Electric and hybrid vehicles

### Answers to frequently asked questions on electric and hybrid vehicles.

This content is for general information only. It does not, and is not intended to, provide legal or technical advice or to represent a legal interpretation of the matters it addresses.

* * *

## Frequently asked questions

[What is an electric or e-car?](http://www.rsa.ie/road-safety/road-users/car-drivers/resources/electric-and-hybrid-vehicles#)

It is powered by one or more electric motors using electrical energy stored in rechargeable batteries or another energy storage device.

[What is a hybrid car?](http://www.rsa.ie/road-safety/road-users/car-drivers/resources/electric-and-hybrid-vehicles#)

It uses more than one means of propulsion. At the moment, that means combining a normal petrol or diesel engine with an electric motor.

[What is a hybrid electric car (HEV)?](http://www.rsa.ie/road-safety/road-users/car-drivers/resources/electric-and-hybrid-vehicles#)

It is a cross between a hybrid car and an e-car so has both electric and conventional means of power.

[What is a plug-in hybrid car?](http://www.rsa.ie/road-safety/road-users/car-drivers/resources/electric-and-hybrid-vehicles#)

It is a hybrid car which provides a way to plug the battery into an electrical outlet for recharging while parked.

[Are e-cars or hybrid cars safe?](http://www.rsa.ie/road-safety/road-users/car-drivers/resources/electric-and-hybrid-vehicles#)

Electric vehicles (EVs) must undergo the same rigorous safety testing and meet the same safety standards required for conventional vehicles sold in Europe as well as EV specific standards for limiting chemical spillage from batteries, securing batteries during a crash, and isolating the chassis from the high-voltage system to prevent electric shock. In addition, one safety concern specific to electric vehicles is their silent operation so vulnerable road users such as pedestrians or cyclists may be less likely to hear an electric vehicle than a conventional vehicle.New electric vehicles now must have an acoustic alerting system onboard.

[What is acoustic vehicle alerting system (AVAS) on electric vehicles?](http://www.rsa.ie/road-safety/road-users/car-drivers/resources/electric-and-hybrid-vehicles#)

Electric and electric-hybrid vehicles which are almost silent particularly at low speeds present a danger to vulnerable road users, e.g., cyclists and pedestrians, particularly those with visual or hearing impairments. AVAS emits an automated sound to alert vulnerable road users of the approaching vehicle.

[Do all electric or hybrid vehicles have to be fitted with AVAS?](http://www.rsa.ie/road-safety/road-users/car-drivers/resources/electric-and-hybrid-vehicles#)

Under EU Regulation [(EU) No 540/2014 Open in new browser tab Open in new browser tab](https://eur-lex.europa.eu/legal-content/EN/TXT/?uri=CELEX%3A32014R0540), the fitment of AVAS to pure electric and hybrid electric vehicles became mandatory on 01 July 2019 for new types of vehicles and on 01 July 2021 for all new vehicles. Any AVAS fitted voluntarily ahead of these dates needed to comply with the applicable requirements as set out in this regulation.

[Under what conditions is AVAS activated?](http://www.rsa.ie/road-safety/road-users/car-drivers/resources/electric-and-hybrid-vehicles#)

AVAS is activated when the motor vehicle is travelling up to approximately 20 km/h and during reversing.

[Can AVAS be switched off?](http://www.rsa.ie/road-safety/road-users/car-drivers/resources/electric-and-hybrid-vehicles#)

Some vehicle models have a pause function. However, on these models, the AVAS will be reactivated each time the vehicle starts up.

[What are the sound limits that need to be met?](http://www.rsa.ie/road-safety/road-users/car-drivers/resources/electric-and-hybrid-vehicles#)

The minimum sound level requirements in dB(A) are provided in the table below:

|  |  |
| --- | --- |
| Constant speed test (10km/h) | 50 dB(A) |
| Constant speed test (20km/h) | 56 dB(A) |
| Reversing test | 47 dB(A) |

[Can I convert a petrol-powered car to an electric car?](http://www.rsa.ie/road-safety/road-users/car-drivers/resources/electric-and-hybrid-vehicles#)

You will need to contact original manufacturer or authorised distributor.

[What are alternatively fuelled vehicles (AFVs)?](http://www.rsa.ie/road-safety/road-users/car-drivers/resources/electric-and-hybrid-vehicles#)

These are vehicles which use non-traditional fuel sources as a means of powering them which include:

| Alternatively Fuelled Vehicles | Source of power |
| --- | --- |
| Electric | Electricity consumed |
| Gas | Hydrogen, natural gas including bio-methane, compressed natural gas (CNG) |
| Liquid | Liquified natural gas (LNG), liquid petroleum gas (LPG) |
| Mechanical | On-board storage or sources including waste heat |

[What regulations govern electric cars or motorbikes used on Irish roads?](http://www.rsa.ie/road-safety/road-users/car-drivers/resources/electric-and-hybrid-vehicles#)

e-Cars and motorbikes or hybrid versions are all considered to be mechanically propelled vehicles and therefore must adhere to the same regulations* that apply to other Irish vehicles.
