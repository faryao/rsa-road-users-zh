# Towing a broken-down vehicle

### Information and answers to frequently asked question relating to towing a broken-down vehicle.

This content is for general information only. It does not, and is not intended to, provide legal or technical advice or to represent a legal interpretation of the matters it addresses.

* * *

## Frequently asked questions

### Towing a broken-down vehicle

[What licence is required to tow a broken-down vehicle?](http://www.rsa.ie/road-safety/road-users/professional-drivers/vehicle-safety-legislation/light-trailers/towing-a-broken-down-vehicle#)

[Can I use my vehicle to tow a broken-down vehicle?](http://www.rsa.ie/road-safety/road-users/professional-drivers/vehicle-safety-legislation/light-trailers/towing-a-broken-down-vehicle#)

Yes, but only to bring it to the nearest place of safety or repair.However, it should only be towed in the event that no professional towing service is available.

[What is the definition of drawbar?](http://www.rsa.ie/road-safety/road-users/professional-drivers/vehicle-safety-legislation/light-trailers/towing-a-broken-down-vehicle#)

Any drawbar, chain or other towing device including any special fitting for the attachment of such drawbar, chain, or towing device.

[What is the definition of a broken-down vehicle?](http://www.rsa.ie/road-safety/road-users/professional-drivers/vehicle-safety-legislation/light-trailers/towing-a-broken-down-vehicle#)

A vehicle which has broken-down on the road and needs to be towed. However, it should only be towed in the event that no professional towing service is available.

[Can I legally tow a vehicle using a spectacle frame?](http://www.rsa.ie/road-safety/road-users/professional-drivers/vehicle-safety-legislation/light-trailers/towing-a-broken-down-vehicle#)

Yes. The towed vehicle will be treated as a trailer. However, regulations for lifting devices fall under the remit of the [HSA](http://www.hsa.ie/eng/) and you should contact them for more information.

[Is it legal to use a rope, wire rope or chain when towing a broken-down vehicle?](http://www.rsa.ie/road-safety/road-users/professional-drivers/vehicle-safety-legislation/light-trailers/towing-a-broken-down-vehicle#)

There is no law against towing a broken-down vehicle using a rope or chain, provided it is strong enough to withstand the demands of towing a vehicle and it is only used to bring the broken-down vehicle to the nearest place of safety or repair.

[What if there is no garage nearby?](http://www.rsa.ie/road-safety/road-users/professional-drivers/vehicle-safety-legislation/light-trailers/towing-a-broken-down-vehicle#)

If the broken-down vehicle is blocking traffic or likely to cause an accident, it should immediately be towed to the nearest place of safety or repair. If you are unable to tow the vehicle, contact a recovery operator who will transport the vehicle safely there.

[Can a broken-down vehicle be towed if it is punctured?](http://www.rsa.ie/road-safety/road-users/professional-drivers/vehicle-safety-legislation/light-trailers/towing-a-broken-down-vehicle#)

Yes, but the speed must not exceed 32 km/h and must only be towed to the nearest convenient place of safety or repair.

[Can I tow a broken-down vehicle on the motorway?](http://www.rsa.ie/road-safety/road-users/professional-drivers/vehicle-safety-legislation/light-trailers/towing-a-broken-down-vehicle#)

We advise against this. If a vehicle breaks down on the motorway, try to move it to the hard shoulder and switch on your hazard lights and contact a vehicle recovery operator.

[Is there a speed limit for towing a broken-down vehicle?](http://www.rsa.ie/road-safety/road-users/professional-drivers/vehicle-safety-legislation/light-trailers/towing-a-broken-down-vehicle#)

A vehicle towing, whether it be a trailer or broken-down vehicle must not exceed 80 km/h. If a lower speed limit sign is posted this must be obeyed at all times.

### Towing a vehicle with an A-frame or towing dolly

[What is an A-frame and towing dolly?](http://www.rsa.ie/road-safety/road-users/professional-drivers/vehicle-safety-legislation/light-trailers/towing-a-broken-down-vehicle#)

Towing Devices Description
A-frame This is a lightweight frame attached from the towing points of a towed vehicle to the ball type coupling on a towing vehicle.
A Towing dolly This is a lightweight trailer-like frame with wheels, ramps and winch which is attached to the coupling of a towing vehicle and used to elevate and transport a vehicle.

[Can I use an A-frame, towing dolly, or drawbar to tow a broken-down vehicle?](http://www.rsa.ie/road-safety/road-users/professional-drivers/vehicle-safety-legislation/light-trailers/towing-a-broken-down-vehicle#)

Yes, but by law towing a broken-down vehicle is the same as towing a trailer so the following rules apply:

*   Carrying capacity: The weight of the broken-down vehicle cannot be more than the carrying capacity of the towing device if one is used e.g. A-Frame, towing dolly, drawbar etc.
*   Towing capacity: The weight of both the broken-down vehicle and the towing device cannot be more than the manufacturer’s rated towing capacity for your vehicle.
*   Licence: The driver of the towing vehicle must be the holder of category BE licence and the driver steering the broken-down vehicle must also hold the appropriate licence for that vehicle.

[Can I use an A-frame to tow a car behind a motorhome?](http://www.rsa.ie/road-safety/road-users/professional-drivers/vehicle-safety-legislation/light-trailers/towing-a-broken-down-vehicle#)

Yes. But you must hold an appropriate driving licence and ensure that the vehicle’s towing capacity is high enough to tow this weight.

[Can I use a braked A-frame to tow my car?](http://www.rsa.ie/road-safety/road-users/professional-drivers/vehicle-safety-legislation/light-trailers/towing-a-broken-down-vehicle#)

Yes. Provided your towing vehicle has a sufficient towing capacity and you hold the relevant category BE driving licence. Contact vehicle manufacturer to obtain the towing capacity for your vehicle.

[Can I use A-frame on a motorway and if so, what is the maximum speed for towing?](http://www.rsa.ie/road-safety/road-users/professional-drivers/vehicle-safety-legislation/light-trailers/towing-a-broken-down-vehicle#)

Yes. The maximum legal speed limit for towing an A-frame on a motorway is 80 km/h. If a lower speed limit sign is posted then this must be obeyed at all times. You must also hold the appropriate driving licence.

[Do I have to stay in the left lane when towing on a motorway?](http://www.rsa.ie/road-safety/road-users/professional-drivers/vehicle-safety-legislation/light-trailers/towing-a-broken-down-vehicle#)

Yes. You must always stay in the left lane. You should not enter the right lane or lane nearest the central median unless there is an obstruction in the left lane and if so, you must warn traffic accordingly.

[When using an A-frame or towing dolly, does the vehicle that is being towed require motor tax and NCT?](http://www.rsa.ie/road-safety/road-users/professional-drivers/vehicle-safety-legislation/light-trailers/towing-a-broken-down-vehicle#)

No.
