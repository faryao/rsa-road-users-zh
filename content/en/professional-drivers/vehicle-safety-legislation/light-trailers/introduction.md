# Introduction to light trailers

### Important vehicle standards for domestic and light trailers.

Whether you tow a trailer for work or domestic reasons, it’s important to know your legal obligations and ensure that you do not drive a vehicle or tow a trailer that your driving licence may not cover.

This content is for general information only. It does not, and is not intended to, provide legal or technical advice or to represent a legal interpretation of the matters it addresses.

The differences between a light and heavy trailer:

![Image 10: horse-box](http://www.rsa.ie/images/default-source/2-up-layout-(350x306)/horse-box.jpg?Status=Master&sfvrsn=fff37e9a_1)

A**light trailer** has a design gross vehicle weight (DGVW) of 3,500 kg or less.

![Image 11: articulated-trailer](http://www.rsa.ie/images/default-source/2-up-layout-(350x306)/articulated-trailer.jpg?Status=Master&sfvrsn=51fbcb38_1)

A**[heavy trailer](http://www.rsa.ie/road-safety/road-users/professional-drivers/vehicle-safety-legislation/heavy-trailers)**has a design gross vehicle weight (DGVW) of more than 3,500 kg.

* * *

## Light trailers

**Light trailers fall into two categories:**

![Image 12: car_trailer](http://www.rsa.ie/images/default-source/2-up-layout-(350x306)/car_trailer.jpg?Status=Master&sfvrsn=fff852ae_1)

01 - Trailers with a maximum mass not exceeding 750 kg, e.g. small car trailers.

![Image 13: jeep-and-trailer](http://www.rsa.ie/images/default-source/2-up-layout-(350x306)/jeep-and-trailerbfdc0c9c-58b4-4147-97e2-09741260cf59.jpg?Status=Master&sfvrsn=327b8040_1)

02- Trailers with a maximum mass exceeding 750 kg but not exceeding 3,500 kg, e.g. larger general duty trailers including flatbed or plant trailers, car transporters, most trailer caravans, horse boxes and livestock trailers.
